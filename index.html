Young people have the power to save the planet...

Youth Climate Action Now

Share your voice with the world!

As youth, our voice is underrepresented and under requested in politcs and every other area of society. Yet, voices of youth continue to to be the most powerful and represent the most ambitous generation yet. It is our responsibility to show the world that we care about the climate and that they should too. Youth Climate Action Now (YouCAN) Corvallis is a campaign that involves youth in policy making and climate activism--leveraging our talents, enthusiasm, and love for the world to shape policy decisions on a local, county, and state level.

Who We Are

Our Leadership Team

Co-Chair

Debra Higbee-Sudyka

The Chair of the Corvallis Environmental Action Team of the Sierra Club, Debra has experience in local and state policy making. As the main proponent of the Plastic Bag Ban, she has made fundamental change in our community.

Co-Chair

Aaron Kratzer

Even before he graduated from Oregon State in 2014, Aaron was interested in environmental justice and politcal action. He is part of a number of groups in Corvallis that seek to support youth and find justice.

Outreach Coordinator

Sierra Laverty

As a sixth generation Idahoan with a family history of both ranching and conservation, Sierra was raised to be a steward of the land. She brings three years of outreach experience to her current role at YouCAN, and is ecstatic to contribute to the Corvallis community by amplifying the voices of youth and young adults.

Campaign Updates

What have we been up to?

2 February 2016

Tell the world!

The City of Corvallis has formed a group that is working on local climate issues--the Climate Action Task Force (CATF). The YouCAN Corvallis leadership team testified at the CATF meeting about the necessity of legally-binding, science-based emissions reduction targets. Afterward we received positive feedback from many people in the room, and we plan on returning often to these meetings to provide a youth-focused perspective on the CATF's work.
